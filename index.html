Enhance Workplace Safety with Safety Behavioral Observation (SBO)

Empower your team to prioritize safety with SBO. Encourage staff to observe and report any safety concerns they encounter. We'll analyze these reports to identify trends and take proactive measures. Together, let's create a safer work environment.

What is SBO?

Safety Behavioral Observation (SBO) is a proactive approach used here at MAERSK safety management. It involves systematically observing and recording employees' behaviors to identify unsafe practices and potential hazards. By focusing on behaviors rather than outcomes, SBO aims to create a proactive safety culture where potential issues are addressed before they result in accidents or injuries.

Our Objective/Purpose

1. Eliminate accidents and negative incidences of any kind

2. Promote safety awareness to all employees

3. Design supporting programs to address safety issues raised in SBO/QBO

4.To intervene to reinforce safe behavior or change unsafe behavior or conditions

Who?

Safety Officers

Is in charge of collecting all SBO forms and classify them based on their categories; in charge of monitoring the SBOs submitted. Monitor, review and analyze all collected data. Design action points to prevent if not to eliminate reoccurrence of the unsafe behavior and quality violations.

Policies

MAERSK and 3rd party service provider personnel must comply with the following safety requirements:

⁍ Maintain full responsibility and liability for all environmental, safety, security, health, and quality compliance matters.

⁍ Each personnel are responsible for correcting environmental, safety, security, health, and quality violations and/or unsafe conditions present in his/her operation.

⁍ Site/Safety Managers, Safety Officers, Supervisors, and workers must ensure that all work activities and/or services are always carried out safely to prevent injuries and damage to properties.

⁍ All warehouse personnel are required to submit SBO/QBO to HSSE department.

MAERSK employee = 2 SBO/QBO per week

3rd party service provider = 1 SBO/QBO per week

⁍ Reporting of all SBO/QBO submitted will be reported in HSSE meeting; All violations observed or reported must be assessed, classified and disciplinary actions must be processed in accordance with table of offense of each party.

⁍ Only up to 3 minor violations will be indulged per employee.

First Violation – Verbal warning, call the attention of violators and explain the safety rules and regulation.

Second Violation – written explanation (Malayang Salaysay) and confiscate company I.D or Safety Badge then re-orient safety rules and regulation

Third Violation – Subject for disciplinary action (Based on Code of discipline of each party)

⁍ When conducting SBO/QBO, work will immediate be stopped and go to the safest place to conduct SBO/QBO

⁍ Do not be arrogant to violator/s while conducting SBO/QBO

⁍ Violator/s must acknowledge their violation and signed to SBO/QBO form.

⁍ All SBO/QBO must be documented with an SBO/QBO form or thru Viber (with pictures) and submit to safety officer.

⁍ Safety officers ensure that the SBO/QBO form are always available at frisking area.

SBO Process

Observation

Trained observers watch employees as they perform their tasks, noting both safe and unsafe behaviors.

Recording

Observers document their findings, including details about the behaviors observed and the context in which they occurred.

Feedback

Observers provide immediate, constructive feedback to employees, reinforcing safe practices and addressing unsafe behaviors.

Analysis

Data from observations are analyzed to identify trends, common hazards, and areas for improvement.

Action

Based on the analysis, targeted interventions, training programs, and changes to procedures or policies are implemented to enhance overall safety.
